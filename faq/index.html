General

What is Timerdoro?

Timerdoro is a Pomodoro timer for iPhone, iPad, and Mac. The Pomodoro Technique is a popular productivity method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s: you work in focused 25-minute intervals, take a 5-minute break, and after four sessions take a longer 15–30 minute break. The structured rhythm helps you stay focused, avoid burnout, and makes large tasks feel more manageable.

It's a deliberately simple app — no task management, no team features, no AI. Just a beautifully built timer that does one job exceptionally well.

Is Timerdoro free?

Yes — Timerdoro is free to download and use. The free version includes the full Pomodoro timer with all three modes, custom intervals, sounds, notifications, and basic statistics.

Pro is an optional upgrade for €3.99 (one-time, lifetime) that adds themes, advanced statistics, daily goals, custom intervals, and more.

Which devices are supported?

Timerdoro runs natively on iPhone (iOS 17+), iPad (iPadOS 17+), and Mac (macOS 13+). Each platform gets a UI tailored to its screen size and conventions. Test TEST TEST

Apple Watch and macOS Menu Bar support are coming in v1.1. iCloud sync is planned for v1.3.

Yes. Pro is tied to your Apple ID, so once you buy it on one device, you can restore the purchase on any iPhone, iPad, or Mac signed in with the same Apple ID.

In the app: go to Settings, scroll to the bottom, and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you're signed into the App Store with the Apple ID you used to purchase.

Privacy & Data

What data does Timerdoro collect?

None. Timerdoro is fully offline. We don't have servers, we don't run analytics, and we don't ship third-party tracking SDKs.

All your session data is stored locally on your device using Apple's standard storage APIs. Your information never leaves your phone, iPad, or Mac.

Troubleshooting

The timer keeps running when the app is in the background, right?

Yes. Timerdoro uses Date-based timing, so even if iOS pauses the app, the timer is still accurate when you return. A local notification fires when the timer completes regardless of app state.

I don't get notifications when the timer ends. Why?

Most likely you declined notification permissions on first launch. Open iOS Settings → Timerdoro → Notifications and enable them. The timer still works — only the alert in the background depends on this permission.

Why doesn't the alternate app icon change?

Some iOS versions cache app icons aggressively. Try restarting your device. If the issue persists, please contact us with your iOS version.
