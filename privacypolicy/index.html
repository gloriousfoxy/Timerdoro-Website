Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 10, 2026 (English only)

🔒
TL;DR

Timerdoro doesn't collect, sell, or transmit your personal data. Everything stays on your device.

1What Data We Collect

✓Nothing.

Timerdoro is a fully local app. We don't operate servers, we don't have analytics, and we don't run any tracking.

All your data — focus sessions, statistics, settings, themes, tags — is stored exclusively on your device using Apple's standard storage APIs.

2What We Don't Do

  • ✗No accounts, no sign-ups, no logins
  • ✗No third-party analytics (no Firebase, Mixpanel, etc.)
  • ✗No advertising, no ad networks, no ad tracking
  • ✗No data sold or shared with anyone
  • ✗No location tracking
  • ✗No data leaves your device

3In-App Purchases

Timerdoro offers a one-time Pro upgrade through Apple's App Store. The purchase itself is processed by Apple, not by us.

We never see or store your payment information. Apple handles billing, receipts, and refunds according to their Standard Terms.

To verify your Pro status, the app uses Apple's StoreKit framework locally on your device. No purchase data is sent to us.

4System Permissions

Timerdoro requests the following system permissions only when needed:

  • Notifications — to alert you when a timer completes. You can revoke this anytime in iOS Settings.
  • Haptic feedback — to provide gentle touch feedback during timer events. Toggleable in app settings.

No other permissions are requested. No camera, no microphone, no contacts, no photos.

5Children's Privacy

Since Timerdoro doesn't collect any personal information from anyone, it inherently complies with children's privacy laws including COPPA and Apple's Kids Category guidelines.

6Data You Export

Pro users can export their session statistics as a CSV file. This file is generated locally on your device — what happens to it after that (where you save it, who you share it with) is entirely up to you.

We never see this file. It never reaches any server.

7Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes in the future (for example if we ever add an optional cloud-sync feature), we'll update this page and post a notice in the app. The "Last updated" date at the top will reflect the latest revision.

Material changes that affect data handling will require your explicit consent before taking effect.
