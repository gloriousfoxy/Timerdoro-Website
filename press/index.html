Boilerplate Description

Use these short descriptions for articles, listings, or reviews:

Short (50 words)

Timerdoro is a beautifully designed Pomodoro timer for iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Built with privacy at its core — no tracking, no analytics, no subscription. Pay €3.99 once for the Pro upgrade or use the free version forever. Made by an indie developer in Vienna.

Medium (100 words)

Timerdoro is a Pomodoro timer for the Apple ecosystem, built natively with SwiftUI for iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Unlike most productivity apps that demand monthly subscriptions, Timerdoro charges €3.99 once for the optional Pro upgrade — themes, advanced statistics, daily goals, and more. The free tier covers all essential timer functionality. What sets it apart: zero data collection (no analytics, no third parties), adaptive layouts that respect each platform, and a focus on doing one thing exceptionally well. Apple Watch and Menu Bar integrations are coming in v1.1. Created solo by Raphael Wudernitz, a CS student in Vienna, Austria.

Long (200 words)

Timerdoro is a Pomodoro timer designed specifically for Apple devices, built natively with SwiftUI for iPhone, iPad, and Mac. The app focuses on a single goal: helping users focus through structured work intervals — without the bloat of full task-management apps or the friction of subscription models. Free users get the complete Pomodoro experience: customizable intervals, three modes (Focus, Short Break, Long Break), notifications, sounds, and basic statistics. The optional Pro upgrade — a one-time €3.99 purchase rather than recurring fee — unlocks themes, advanced analytics including a 12-week heatmap, daily goals, tags, custom intervals up to 180 minutes, and CSV export. Privacy is treated as a feature, not an afterthought. Timerdoro collects zero user data, runs no analytics, and ships without third-party SDKs. Everything stays on the user's device. The app introduces a thoughtful "End Early" feature: if users finish their task before the timer runs out, they can mark the session as complete after 80% of the focus time has passed — preserving the Pomodoro discipline while accommodating real-world flexibility. Apple Watch and macOS Menu Bar support are scheduled for v1.1. iCloud sync is planned for v1.3.
